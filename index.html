Training Splits and Which One Suits Your Needs

Bro Split

This Split is suited for people who want to live in the gym but still have weekends, and consists of training each body part once a day. Monday may be reserved for only chest training, followed on Tuesday by back, and so on so forth. The pros of this training style are it ensures that the muscle group in question is thuroughly activated, however the cons are that you will have large gaps between working each muscle group that may not be ideal for hypertrophy.

Example

Monday Tuesday Wednesday Thursday Friday Saturday Sunday
Chest Back Legs Arms Shoulders Rest Rest

Push Pull Legs (PPL)

This Split is suited for people who have more time to go to the gym each week and want to maximize the frequency at which they hit each muscle group. The order of the push, pull , and leg split is not set in stone, meaning it can take the form of legs, pull, push if desired. The pros of this training style are its frequency, and guranteed muscle recovery of 48 hours, however the cons are you will have to frequent the gym more often and lose your weekends.

Example

Monday Tuesday Wednesday Thursday Friday Saturday Sunday
Chest/Triceps Back/Biceps Legs/Shoulders Rest Chest/Triceps Back/Biceps Legs/Shoulders

Upper Lower

This Split is suited for people who are willing to put more work into their gym sessions while also putting more emphais on legs, and frequency. This split allows for one day to be used for the desired upper body exercises, typically one or two compound movements with two to three accessories. Followed by a day for the desired low body exercises using the same methodoly mentioned before for the exercise selection. The pros of this training style is the increased in frequency for lower body movements which may interest people trying to fill out their legs, coupled with having weekends. The cons are the gym sessions themselves must be longer to account for the needed weekly volume.

Example

Monday Tuesday Wednesday Thursday Friday Saturday Sunday
Upper Lower Rest Upper Lower Rest Rest
